About the Forum
01
The Concept
A wind that has always connected two worlds
The Sirocco is a wind born deep in the Sahara Desert. It travels north across the Mediterranean Sea and arrives on the shores of Europe ‚Äî crossing borders, carrying warmth, and connecting two worlds long before any diplomatic channel existed. It has done this for thousands of years.
The Sirocco Forum carries the same logic. It is a boutique, invitation-only annual platform that brings together leaders from the EU, the Mediterranean basin, and Africa around three themes that will define the next decade: energy transition, trade and investment, and the digital economy. Youth is not a side event ‚Äî young professionals are seated at every panel table alongside ambassadors and senior leaders.
Why Warsaw?
Poland is the 5th largest economy in the EU and a rising hub for energy policy, agri-tech, and defence. While Western Europe has established channels with North Africa and the Mediterranean, Central and Eastern Europe remains an untapped partner. The Sirocco Forum activates this connection from one of Europe's most prestigious academic campuses.
Why Now?
A gap no other platform fills
No existing European forum places Central Europe, the Mediterranean, and Africa in the same conversation. The Sirocco Forum is the first platform to do so.

I
Energy & The Green Transition
North Africa holds some of the world's greatest solar and wind potential. Central Europe urgently needs to diversify its energy supply. This panel explores how the EU, the Mediterranean, and Africa can build a shared energy architecture ‚Äî from LNG supply today to green hydrogen infrastructure tomorrow.

Trade, Investment & The African Market
The African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA) is the largest free trade zone in the world by number of countries. This panel examines concrete business opportunities for European companies seeking African market access, and for African investors seeking European technology and capital.

Digital Economy & Technology Transfer
From data infrastructure to startup ecosystems, the digital economy is the fastest-growing arena for Euro-African cooperation. This panel discusses digital sovereignty, critical raw materials, cybersecurity cooperation, and how African startups and European tech firms can build lasting joint ventures.
